Our Story

We just miss being around good people.

Over the past several years, many of us have quietly left churches and communities that no longer felt like home. We watched leaders we trusted lose their way, and the spaces that once brought us together started to feel more divisive than unifying.

Salt & Light Social exists because we believe community shouldn't be this hard to find. We're not a church, not a ministry, not a political group. We're just people who love God, love humanity, and want somewhere safe to laugh, create, and belong again.

If you have a good heart and you've been looking for your people, you just found them.

What We Believe

Built on What Actually Matters

Love First

We lead with empathy, kindness, and grace. Every person who walks through our doors is met with genuine warmth. No judgment, no agenda.

Safe Space

We're here to connect over what brings us together, not what tears us apart. Leave the noise at the door.

Real Community

Not surface-level small talk. We want people who actually want to know each other, show up for each other, and do life together.

Good Questions, Real Answers

Nope. Salt & Light Social is purely a social community. We're faith-rooted people, but this isn't a church, a bible study, or a ministry. Think of it as a group of good humans who happen to love God and want to have fun together.

You just have to be a good person with a kind heart. Our community is rooted in Christian values of love and compassion, but we welcome anyone who vibes with that energy and wants genuine human connection.

It means this is a space free from the political culture that has divided so many faith communities. We're not a political group and we don't talk politics at our events. We just want people who believe in loving their neighbor, period. If that's you, you belong here.

We're starting in central Orange County and will organize meetups across different cities depending on where our members are. When you sign up, tell us your area and we'll connect you with nearby groups.
